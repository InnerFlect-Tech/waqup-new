# Our Story Page — Rewrite Plan

**Purpose**: Transform the waQup "Our History" page into a powerful founder story that builds trust, emotional resonance, and credibility.

**Status**: Plan complete → Implementation in progress

---

## Research Summary

### Founder Story Best Practices
- **Authenticity over perfection**: Raw, vulnerable narratives build more trust than polished messaging
- **Focus on user problems**: Center story on user's pain and decision-making needs; founder credibility connects to customer problem
- **Placement**: Founder story works after users understand what the product does — Our Story is a dedicated page, so visitors arrive knowing they want the founder narrative
- **Emotional connection**: Creates resonance that improves engagement and conversion
- **Avoid**: Hype language, exaggerated startup tropes, vague outcomes, formal/corporate tone

### Calm, Notion, Headspace Patterns
- **Calm**: "Meditation techniques incredibly valuable… lacked great vehicles for mainstream" — problem clarity before solution
- **Notion**: Near-bankruptcy, rebuild from scratch, philosophy-driven (Engelbart's "augmenting human intellect")
- **Common**: Personal discovery → failure of existing solutions → insight → persistence → product as philosophy

---

## waQup Founder Context

**Core discovery**: Speaking affirmations in your own voice is far more powerful than listening to others.

**Why generic tools failed**: Most meditation/affirmation apps give you someone else's voice telling you what to believe. Personal transformation requires personal language. The real power is hearing your own voice reinforcing your identity.

**Manual process before waQup**: Write scripts → structure practices → record manually → organize repetition. Effective but cumbersome; not accessible to most people.

**waQup simplifies**: Guides you through creating affirmations, meditations, and rituals in your voice. Focus on practice, not process.

**Philosophy themes**:
- Self-authorship
- Identity creation
- Subconscious reprogramming
- Personal responsibility
- Clarity of intention
- Tools that empower people rather than control them

## Narrative Structure (5 Sections)

### Section 1 — Personal Origin
**Headline**: "This started as something I needed myself."

- How the founder discovered the power of hearing their own voice
- Why generic tools didn't work for them
- What the manual process looked like before waQup (scripts, recording, Ableton, etc.)
- **Tone**: Intimate, reflective

### Section 2 — The Realization
**Key insight**: Transformation happens when you speak your own intentions.

- Most tools tell you what to believe
- waQup lets you encode your own beliefs
- Short, powerful statements

### Section 3 — Why waQup Exists
- waQup simplifies the process
- Instead of scripting, recording, organizing manually → system guides you
- Emphasize: personal voice, simplicity, accessibility

### Section 4 — What waQup Represents
- Position waQup as philosophy, not just app
- Tools should help people express themselves
- Technology should empower inner clarity
- Identity can be shaped intentionally
- **Tone**: Thoughtful but concise

### Section 5 — Founder Message
- Direct, personal voice
- Example direction: "waQup exists because something simple changed my life, and I believe more people should have access to it."
- Avoid formal
- Name: Daniel Indias Fernandes
