## Summary

| Page | Route | Purpose | When to Use |
|------|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Our Story** | `/our-story` | Founder narrative — trust, legitimacy, why we exist | Visitors evaluating credibility, investors, curious about who's behind waQup |

## Research-Backed Best Practices

### Our Story ( founder narrative )

**Visitor intent**: Legitimacy, expertise, trust before purchase. About pages rank top 10 most visited; 1–2 min avg engagement.

**Best practices** (River, Cobloom, GrowthMentor):
- **Lead with problem**, not founding date: "After observing [customer problem], we knew there had to be a better way"
- **Structure**: problem discovery → failed solutions → insight moment → your approach
- **Founder credibility**: domain expertise that connects to the problem
- **Values through evidence**: "We value X. That's why we do Y."
- **Soft CTAs**: "Want to see if we're a good fit?" at natural transition points
- **Authentic visuals**: real photos, not stock

### The Science ( mechanism explanation )

**Visitor intent**: Proof, reassurance, understanding *why* this works. For novel products (voice-based subconscious rewiring), many visitors need mechanistic credibility.

**Best practices**:
- Explain the mechanism (subconscious, neuroplasticity, own-voice research)
- Use concrete research references where possible
- Keep structure: problem → science → solution
- CTA to How It Works or onboarding for those convinced

### How It Works

**Purpose**: Remove ambiguity about what happens next. Steps to become a customer. Clarifies process.

**Best practices** (Cardsetter, B12):
- 3–4 primary steps
- "What happens after I sign up?"
- Great for novel products where process is unfamiliar

## Conversion Flow

```
Landing → How It Works (process) ─┬→ The Science (proof) ─→ Sign up
                                   └→ Our Story (trust) ───→ Sign up
```

- **How It Works** answers: "What do I do?"
- **The Science** answers: "Why does this work?"
- **Our Story** answers: "Who are you and why should I trust you?"
